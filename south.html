    • The South of Argentina.

    The southern region of Argentina is a place of contrasts and unique beauty. Here, culture intertwines with the vast nature that characterizes this region. Indigenous influence and the presence of native communities are essential elements of southern culture. The Mapuche people are an example of the cultural heritage that has endured through generations, and their traditions, such as craftsmanship and herbal medicine, remain an integral part of local life.

    When it comes to gastronomy, the southern zone is famous for its emphasis on seafood. Fresh seafood and fish are standout dishes, and the king crab, a giant species of crab, is a regional delicacy. Additionally, Argentine Patagonia is known for its slow-cooked asados and lamb, prepared on a cross-shaped grill. The region also offers a variety of wild fruits, like calafates, which are used in delicious sweets and liquors.

    The flora and fauna of the southern zone are truly impressive. Here, Patagonia dazzles with its landscapes of mountains, glaciers, and vast steppes. The region is home to a rich wildlife, with emblematic species such as the guanaco, the Andean condor, and the puma. National parks, such as Los Glaciares National Park, provide opportunities for observing icebergs and majestic glaciers in constant motion.

    The southern region of Argentina is a haven of authenticity and pristine nature, where culture, gastronomy, and biodiversity come together in a stunning spectacle that transports you to a world of wonders.
